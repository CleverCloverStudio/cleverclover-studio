# Pricing Psychology Redesign — Complete Instructions for Claude Code

## Overview
Redesign how pricing is presented across the entire CleverClover.studio site. The goal is to shift visitor perception from "this looks expensive" to "I could actually hire this guy." The brand should feel premium, fast, accessible, and better value than an agency — not cheap, not discount, not bargain. Think Apple meets boutique creative studio meets startup-friendly execution partner.

Do NOT change the actual service offerings or remove premium options. This is a FRAMING redesign, not a price cut.

Apply these changes to every page that displays pricing: /services/video, /services/audio, /services/music, /services/web, /packages, and the homepage.

---

## STRATEGY 1: Lead With "Starting At" Language

Every pricing display should lead with the lowest entry point. Replace all price ranges that show "Price — $X,XXX" or "$500 — $2,500" formats.

**Old format (remove everywhere):**
"Brand / promo video (1-3 min): $500 – $1,200"

**New format (use everywhere):**
"Brand / promo video (1-3 min) — starting at $500"

The high end of the range should either be removed entirely or shown only in smaller muted text below as "Custom quotes for complex projects" — never as the primary visible number.

Apply this "starting at" language to every single pricing card, pricing line, and pricing table across the entire site.

## STRATEGY 4: Add Context to Premium Prices

Anywhere a price of $2,000 or above appears on the site, add a brief "what's included" breakdown below it. A number without context feels arbitrary and expensive. A number with a detailed scope feels like a deal.

For example, the Full Brand Build at $4,000+ should always show the itemized list of deliverables (website, video, music, social content, voiceover, SEO, support, revisions). When someone sees 8+ deliverables for $4,000, the mental math shifts from "that's expensive" to "that's actually a lot for the money."

---

## STRATEGY 5: Add a "Projects Start at $75" Banner

On the homepage, add a subtle but visible line near the hero or in the services overview section:

"Projects starting at $75 — from podcast edits to full brand builds."

This immediately reframes the entire site. Before they've scrolled to any pricing page, they already know the floor is $75, not $7,500. This single line will dramatically reduce bounce rate from price shock.

Place it either:
- Below the hero CTA buttons, as a small muted text line
- Or at the top of the services overview section as an introductory line

Keep it subtle and elegant — not a banner ad, not a sale sign. Just a quiet, confident statement.

---

## STRATEGY 6: Visual De-emphasis of Large Numbers

For all pricing cards across the site:

- The "starting at" label should be in small, muted text (the gold accent color or cream at reduced opacity)
- The price number itself should be medium weight, not extra bold
- The service name/description should be the visually dominant element, not the price
- Premium tier cards should have a slightly more muted background or border compared to starter/popular tiers
- The most affordable options should have the most visual prominence (slightly larger cards, or a subtle highlight)

Do NOT make this look like a discount or sale. The visual hierarchy should simply guide the eye to accessible options first, with premium options available but not screaming.
